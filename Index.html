Why Vilnius?

Our Book Club Format

At Ctrl + Alt + Read, we believe in:

  • Inclusivity: All reading levels and preferences welcome
  • Respect: All opinions are valid and valued
  • Engagement: Active participation makes meetings more fun
  • Growth: Reading outside our comfort zones

We meet monthly, with PDF copies shared in our community group.

Bring your thoughts, questions, and an open mind!

Today's Book

The Midnight Library

by Matt Haig

Published in 2020

Winner of Goodreads Choice Award for Fiction

A novel about regret, possibility, and the infinite potential of life

"Between life and death there is a library, and within that library, the shelves go on forever. Every book provides a chance to try another life you could have lived."

Discussion Questions

  1. What did you think of the concept of the Midnight Library?
  2. How did Nora's character development throughout the novel resonate with you?
  3. Which of Nora's parallel lives was most interesting to you, and why?
  4. How did the book's exploration of regret affect your perspective on your own choices?
  5. Did you find the ending satisfying? Why or why not?
  6. What do you think Matt Haig is trying to say about happiness and fulfillment?
